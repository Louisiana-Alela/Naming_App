About

In Ghana, an Outdooring is the traditional naming ceremony for infants.
This also symbolized that the child was likely to survive hence was given a name.
In addition to the day name, Ghanians frequently give children a name of an elder relative, either living or deceased.
Duding this ceremony, male infants would be circumcised and female infants would have their ears pierced.
Outdooring serves as a symbolic ceremony and celebration of birth.
The ceremony takes place with the relatives and close friends attending.
Akan is the traditional naming ceremony for infants in Ghanian culture.
